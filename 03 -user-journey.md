## 6. User Journey & Key Insights

This section combines the narrative journey of an SDR using the Signal Activation Layer with core discovery insights about outbound sales behavior.

---

### 6.1 High-Level User Journey

1. SDR logs into Enginy  
2. Reviews signal feed  
3. AI enriches signals with context and recommendations  
4. SDR identifies actionable opportunities  
5. AI generates campaign suggestion  
6. SDR reviews and edits message  
7. SDR launches campaign  
8. Prospect engages (reply / meeting)  
9. Opportunity enters pipeline  

---

### 6.2 Narrative Case – Signal Activation Layer

#### Helping SDRs convert signals into pipeline and revenue

Sales teams operate in environments where timing and relevance are critical.

Although platforms like Enginy provide intent signals, many of these signals are not effectively converted into outreach actions.

This project explores how an AI-powered layer can transform signals into high-probability outbound opportunities.

---

### 1. Problem

Modern outbound tools generate a large number of intent signals, such as job changes, hiring activity, and company updates.

However, having access to signals does not guarantee action.

SDRs must manually interpret signals, decide whether they are relevant, and create outreach campaigns. This creates friction, delays, and missed opportunities.

Since SDR performance is measured by pipeline generation and conversion, this gap directly impacts business outcomes.

As a result, outbound execution becomes inconsistent, slow, and dependent on individual judgment.

---

### 2. Discovery

This case study is based on product analysis and common patterns observed in outbound sales workflows.

Several patterns emerged during discovery.

---

#### Signal Overload

Users are exposed to many signals but lack guidance on how to act.

- Signals are visible but not actionable  
- Users must interpret them manually  

---

#### Decision Friction

SDRs spend time deciding instead of executing.

- unclear prioritization  
- lack of context  
- hesitation before acting  

---

#### Time Sensitivity

Signals lose value quickly.

- opportunities are time-sensitive  
- delayed outreach reduces response probability  

---

#### Inconsistent Execution

Different SDRs act differently on the same signals.

- reliance on intuition  
- lack of standardized approach  

---

#### Low Trust in Signals

Users may ignore signals due to uncertainty.

- unclear relevance  
- lack of explanation  
- inconsistent results  

---

### 3. Key Insights

Discovery revealed several important insights.

---

#### Signals do not create value — action does

The presence of signals alone does not improve performance.

Value is only created when signals are converted into outreach.

---

#### Speed directly impacts conversion

The faster a signal is acted upon, the higher the probability of response.

---

#### Decision-making is the main bottleneck

The biggest friction is not execution, but deciding:

- whether to act  
- how to act  

---

#### Consistency drives performance

Standardizing how signals are used can improve team-wide results.

---

#### Quality of opportunities matters more than quantity

Users benefit more from a small set of actionable opportunities than a large set of raw signals.

---

### 4. Product Opportunity

Existing tools focus on:

- generating signals  
- enabling campaign execution  

However, they do not connect the two effectively.

This creates an opportunity for a **Signal Activation Layer** that:

- adds context to signals  
- recommends actions  
- enables immediate execution  

---

### 5. Proposed Solution

The proposed solution combines two components.

---

#### Signal Activation Layer

An intelligent layer that transforms signals into actionable opportunities by adding:

- context ("why this matters")  
- recommended action  
- persona mapping  

---

#### Campaign Enablement

A lightweight system that allows users to:

- generate campaign messages  
- review and edit  
- launch outreach quickly  

---

The system focuses on **increasing conversion by reducing time and friction between signal detection and outreach execution**, while creating a foundation for future automation.
