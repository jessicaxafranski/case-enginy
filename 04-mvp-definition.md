## 4. MVP Definition

This section narrows the product into an MVP focused on increasing conversion by enabling users to act on intent signals and move towards signal-triggered campaigns.

---

### Context

This case study is based on the analysis of outbound sales workflows and Enginy's current product capabilities.

Enginy already provides intent signals and ranking. However, based on internal feedback, these signals are still underutilized and require improvement.

At the same time, the company aims to move towards **automated campaigns triggered by signals**, indicating a clear strategic direction.

Currently, there is a gap between signal detection and outbound execution. Users are exposed to signals but must manually interpret them and create campaigns.

This results in delays, missed opportunities, and lower conversion across the outbound funnel.

---

### Persona Prioritization

To define the MVP scope, different user segments were analyzed based on their role in driving outbound conversion.

| Persona | Main Need | Pain Point | Constraint Type | Impact | Frequency | Priority |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------------|--------|-----------|----------|
| SDRs (Junior) | Clear guidance on what to do | Don’t understand signals, hesitate to act | Cognitive constraint | High | High | High |
| SDRs (Experienced) | Speed and efficiency | Time lost analyzing signals manually | Time constraint | High | High | High |
| Sales Managers | Consistent execution | Team not using signals effectively | Process constraint | Medium | High | Medium |
| Revenue Leaders | Higher conversion | Signals not translating into pipeline | Strategic constraint | High | Medium | High |

The analysis shows that improving how signals are used directly impacts conversion metrics.

Therefore, the MVP focuses on enabling SDRs to act quickly and consistently on signals.

---

### Market Behavior Insight

Signals are available, but action is inconsistent.

Users tend to:

- ignore unclear signals  
- delay outreach due to uncertainty  
- rely on manual judgment  
- miss optimal timing windows  

This creates a gap between **signal availability and conversion outcomes**.

Additionally, fully automated campaigns are not yet feasible due to low trust and signal maturity.

This reinforces the need for a **human-in-the-loop approach as a transition toward automation**.

---

### 4.1 MVP Goal

The goal of the MVP is to increase conversion by reducing the time and effort required to act on signals.

Instead of manually interpreting signals and creating campaigns, users receive clear, guided actions that can be quickly executed.

This also serves as a stepping stone toward future automation.

---

### 4.2 MVP Features

The MVP focuses on enabling signal-to-action conversion and preparing the foundation for automated campaigns.

Core features include:

- *Signal context ("Why this matters")*: Explain the relevance of each signal
- *Suggested action*: Recommend who to contact and how to approach
- *Contact-level mapping*: Identify the right decision-maker
- *Lightweight campaign generation*: Provide ready-to-use outreach messages
- *One-click campaign launch*: Reduce friction in execution
- *Basic prioritization*: Act now / Review / Ignore
- *Signal-to-campaign tracking*: Link signals to campaigns for future automation

---

### 4.3 Features Excluded from MVP

To keep the MVP focused and aligned with signal maturity, several features are excluded.

The MVP prioritizes improving conversion before introducing full automation.

**Full automation**

- Automatic campaign triggering (no human approval)
- Autonomous workflows

**Advanced AI**

- Fully personalized AI-generated campaigns
- Complex decision-making models

**Advanced analytics**

- Full funnel attribution (signal → revenue)
- Predictive scoring models

**Multi-channel execution**

- LinkedIn, calls, SMS campaigns

---

### 4.4 Success Metrics

The success of the MVP will be evaluated based on its impact on outbound conversion.

The primary success metric is the **increase in signal-to-campaign conversion rate**.

| Metric Category | Metric | What it Measures |
|---|---|---|
| Conversion | Signal-to-campaign rate | % of signals that result in outreach |
| Speed | Time from signal to campaign | Reduction in delay between detection and action |
| Engagement | Reply rate vs baseline | Impact of signal-based campaigns |
| Trust | Override rate | User confidence in suggested actions |
| Business Impact | Meetings booked | Contribution to pipeline generation |

These metrics validate whether improving signal usability leads to higher conversion and supports future automation.
