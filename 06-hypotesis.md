## 7. Hypotheses & Validation Plan

This section defines the key hypotheses behind the product and the metrics used to validate them.

---

### 7.1 Core Hypotheses

The MVP is based on the assumption that improving how signals are presented and used will increase outbound conversion.

---

#### Hypothesis 1: Context increases action

> If users understand why a signal matters, they will be more likely to act on it.

**Rationale:**
Signals today are presented without explanation, creating uncertainty and hesitation. Users often ignore signals because they don’t understand their relevance.

**Success Metrics:**
- Signal → campaign conversion rate  
- Click rate on “Generate campaign”  
- Time spent per signal (should decrease)  

---

#### Hypothesis 2: Suggested actions reduce friction

> If users receive clear recommended actions, they will create campaigns faster and more consistently.

**Rationale:**
The main bottleneck in outbound workflows is decision-making. Users spend time figuring out what to do instead of executing.

**Success Metrics:**
- Time from signal to campaign creation  
- Campaigns created per user  
- Drop-off rate (signal viewed → campaign created)  

---

#### Hypothesis 3: Faster action improves conversion

> If outreach happens faster after a signal is detected, reply rates and meetings booked will increase.

**Rationale:**
Signals are time-sensitive. The longer the delay, the lower the probability of response.

**Success Metrics:**
- Time from signal detection → first outreach  
- Reply rate vs baseline (cold outreach)  
- Meetings booked per campaign  

---

### 7.2 Validation Approach

The hypotheses will be validated through an MVP rollout with controlled exposure.

- Compare behavior before vs after feature adoption  
- Track user cohorts (users with vs without feature)  
- Measure impact on conversion metrics  

---

### 7.3 Key Validation Signals

The MVP will be considered successful if:

- Signal → campaign rate increases significantly (>50%)  
- Time to action decreases (<2 hours)  
- Reply rate improves vs baseline (≥1.5×)  
- Meetings booked increase  
- Override rate decreases over time (<30%)  

These indicators validate whether the product effectively reduces friction and improves outbound conversion.
